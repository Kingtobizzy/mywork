Man Utd's slump: Who is to blame? The players? Erik ten Hag? The Glazers? Have your say

With Man Utd's season plunging to a new low after a Carabao Cup fourth-round exit at the hands of Newcastle, we ask who is to blame for the club's slump? The 3-0 defeat at Old Trafford followed the 3-0 humbling at the hands of Manchester rivals Manchester City on Sunday. Defeat last night was United's eighth defeat in 15 matches to progress to the quarter-finals. But who is to blame for the slump? Is it the players? Is it the manager Erik ten Hag? Or is it the Glazers? Or something else?
Speaking after the defeat against Newcastle, Ten Hag said: “We know this is not good enough. We have to take responsibility for it, I have to take responsibility for it. We don't give the performance, so I feel sorry for the fans. It is below our standards and we have to put it right. “We have to recover from it but we have to quickly recover - Saturday is the next game (against Fulham), and we have to raise our standards. This is not good enough.”
